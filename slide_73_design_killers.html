Design Comfort Killers

The inner ear (vestibular system) detects acceleration, not velocity. If your eyes see acceleration but your ear feels nothing, the brain assumes you have been poisoned and induces nausea.
Acceleration
Gradually speeding up or slowing down (like a car) creates a massive sensory conflict. "Ease-in / Ease-out" animations are deadly in VR.
Constant Speed
Move at a fixed velocity instantly (0 to 100 in 1 frame). Or use Teleportation to skip movement entirely.
Smooth Rotation
Slowly rotating the camera creates "Vection" (the illusion that the world is spinning). This is the #1 cause of motion sickness for new users.
Snap Turn
Rotate the camera instantly by 30° or 45°. The brain treats it like a "blink" or a cut, avoiding the motion conflict.
Forced Movement
Taking control of the camera for a "Cutscene", shaking the screen for impact, or moving the head without user input.
Never Lock Head
The user is the camera. Never move it against their will. If you need a cutscene, project it on a virtual 2D screen in front of them.
